Context
The overall funnel shows low reinstatement — and new users reinstate at half the platform average

Across 45,039 first-time soft-restricted sellers on the platform, 48.7% moved to reinstate. But the new user cohort (5,062 sellers) reinstates at only 22% — well below the platform average. The concern raised is whether the 74% who don't reinstate includes high-potential sellers being lost. The data says no.

Finding 01 — Angela's Q2
Cancellation reason is the clearest signal of seller intent — and delayed_order dominates at 60%

Angela's hypothesis is validated. Delayed_order — a passive indicator of inactivity, where the seller simply didn't ship — accounts for 60.5% of soft-restricted new sellers and drives a 10.8% reinstatement rate. Sellers who actively managed their cancellation (item unavailable, changed mind, damaged item) reinstate at 4× the rate and return as active users at 99–100%. The non-moderated comparison cohort shows an identical 67% delayed_order share, confirming moderation is not catching a distinct seller type.

Finding 02 — Angela's Q1
Only 16% receive another order in D30 — and 81% of those cancel again

The D30 cohort data answers Angela's question on pre-cancellation activity directly. Of new sellers who cancel their first order, only 1 in 6 receives another order within 30 days. Of those who do, 81% cancel again — 64% for seller-driven reasons. The delayed_order segment specifically has a 14% D30 reorder rate vs. 20% for active-cancel sellers, and subsequent cancellation rates of 76% vs. 47% respectively.

Assessment & Recommendation
Proceed with 35% rollout — but use two distinct interventions, not one broadcast nudge

Ashika's conclusion to graduate the feature is supported by the data. The refinement: the reinstatement nudge strategy should be segmented by cancellation reason. A single undifferentiated email/push to all 74% non-reinstated sellers will spend the majority of effort on the lowest-intent group (delayed_order, ~61%) where marginal return is minimal.

Data verdict: Non-reinstated sellers show low platform intent before moderation. The cohort not coming back would likely not have generated meaningful order volume regardless. The rollout is not losing good sellers at scale.

delayed_order sellers (~61% of cohort)
Reinstate at 11%. Genuinely low intent. A reinstatement nudge underperforms here. The higher-leverage intervention is upstream: shipping expectation education before the first cancellation, reducing how many enter this bucket.
